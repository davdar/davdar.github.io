• Login to GitHub, or create an account if you don't have one.
  • Navigate to the NIST Privacy Engineering Collaboration Space on GitHub.
  • Click the “Fork” button.
  • Navigate to either “tools” or “use-cases”, depending on the type of your contribution. (Next steps are shown for “tools”.)
  • Navigate to either “de-identification” or “risk-assessment”, depending on the type of your contribution. (Next steps are shown for “de-identification”.)
  • Click the “Create new file” button.
  • Type in the prompt the name of your contribution followed by a “/README.md”. The name you typed before “/” will automatically get added to the path, and the prompt will just show “README.md”. (Next steps are shown for “Duet/README.md”.)
  • Populate the “README.md” below by copying and then completing the relevant template: the tool template or the use case template. (Next steps are shown for Duet contribution.)
  • Click the “Commit new file” button.
  • Navigate up one directory level to either “de-identification” or “risk-assessment”. (Next steps are shown for “de-identification”.)
  • Click the edit button for the top-level “README.md”.
  • Add an entry to this file which reflects your contribution and maintains alphabetical order for all contributions.
  • Click the “Commit changes” button.
  • Navigate to the root of the project “PrivacyEngCollabSpace”.
  • Click the “New pull request” button.
  • Review your contribution and then click the “Create pull request” button.
  • Enter the name of your contribution as the title, and add a short one-sentence description of your contribution, as well as any additional notes; then click the “Create pull request” button.
  • Moderators will now review your pull request and may provide comments or suggestions before it is accepted.
